Hemophilia: Types, Symptoms and Causes

Posted by Suborna Fermi on Jul 26, 2024

Hemophilia, a rare, inherited blood disorder occurs when the blood does not clot as it should. This can lead to spontaneous bleeding after an injury or surgery. Hemophilia happens when a person has low levels of clotting factor. Easy bruising, nosebleeds, and blood in urine or feces are the common signs of hemophilia.

What is hemophilia?

Hemophilia is a rare, inherited blood disorder that affects the body's ability to create blood clots. This can lead to excessive bleeding, both internally and externally, even from minor injuries. The condition is caused by a deficiency in certain clotting factors, which are proteins in the blood that help it to coagulate.

Types of Hemophilia

There are several types of hemophilia, but the two most common are:

  • Hemophilia A: This is the most common type, caused by a deficiency in clotting factor VIII.
  • Hemophilia B: Also known as Christmas disease, this type is caused by a deficiency in clotting factor IX.

Symptoms of Hemophilia

The main symptom of hemophilia is excessive bleeding. Specific symptoms can include:

  • Unexplained and excessive bleeding from cuts or injuries
  • Large or deep bruises
  • Unusual bleeding after vaccinations
  • Pain, swelling or tightness in joints
  • Blood in urine or stool
  • Nosebleeds without a known cause
  • In infants, unexplained irritability

Causes of Hemophilia

Hemophilia is caused by a genetic mutation that affects the body's ability to produce clotting factors. The genes responsible for hemophilia are located on the X chromosome, which is why the condition is more common in males. Females can be carriers of the gene and may experience mild symptoms.
